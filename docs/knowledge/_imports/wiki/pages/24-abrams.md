Abrams is a Hero in Deadlock. He is a hard-boiled, hard-hitting bruiser of a character that focuses on engaging groups of enemies and relying on his high health, high damage output, and life draining ability to stay in the fight. With a powerful area stun and a devastating shoulder charge, Abrams can isolate fragile targets from the rest of the team and set-up his more damaging allies to finish them off.

A capable and well-known detective of New York, Abrams solved crimes ranging from kidnappings to ritual murders. One day, a mysterious Tome was left on his desk with a note of "Don't let them have it" scrawled in Onyx blood; followed by car bombings and break-ins to his office. Now, Abrams is trying to figure out just what the hell is going on.

Case Closed has a 0.7s delay to start reloading after shooting a shell. It takes 0.35s to start reloading and 0.35s to reload each shell. Reloading all shells takes 3.5s.

## Trivia

- Abrams was named Atlas in Neon Prime, and also known as Bull. The icon for Infernal Resilience previously had a bull design.

- Haze calls Abrams a "juggernaut".3] Samoa Joe voiced the character Juggernaut in the "Call of the Bladeform Legacy" arcana voice pack in Dota 2, and later in Dota Underlords.

- The first version of Abrams' Tome in-game used textures from TF2's Bombinomicon.

- In The Binding of Oathkeeper artwork, Abrams has spikes on his back.

- Abrams was the only hero who had unique animations in the shop menu screen. These animations have been removed.

- Abrams was made slightly taller in an unlisted patch change around November 2024.

- Abrams' updated model was first leaked in the 2024-12-06 Update, and later removed. Compared to the final model, this version had slightly different facial hair and still had his old book model.

- "Investigating the Hideout" is displayed as one's status in the in-game Friends List when playing as Abrams in the Hideout.
