A Patron is a deity which takes the form of a large extraplanar entity. Each team’s base houses a Patron, The Hidden King and The Archmother, and victory is secured by defeating the opposing team's Patron, completing the ritual.

The Hidden King appears as a shadowy figure with antlers and glowing yellow eyes, bound to a burning wicker man effigy. Long, claw-like yellow hands extend from the shadows. His base is a factory filled with large speakers and microphones, and his side of the map features working-class red brick buildings labeled "Downtown". The Archmother wears a broken mask resembling the head of the Statue of Liberty, her form bound to a crystalline effigy burning with blue flames. Her base is a museum with a Neoclassic aesthetic, and her side of the map features upper-class white brick buildings labeled "Uptown".

During gameplay, a herald speaks on behalf of the Patrons to their teams, commenting on player performance, announcing major events, and offering boons that allow players to level up.

The two Patrons in-game are the Hidden King and the Archmother. There are more Patrons, but they haven't yet made contact with humanity.5] The motivations of the Hidden King and Archmother are unclear, although The Doorman mentions that they've been waiting to be summoned "for centuries".6] Warden and his family believe the Patrons will "herald the end of humanity" if they are not stopped.7]

Once the ritual is completed, the Patrons can grant one wish to each hero who helped summon them.8] The specifics of the Patron's powers and their limitations are unclear. What the winning Patron intends to do after the ritual is also unclear, although the Patron's herald believes they will usher in a new era.9]10]11]12]

The Patrons have heralds who speak on their behalf.13]14]15]16] The heralds have knowledge on the thoughts and desires of the heroes, and can communicate with them telepathically during the ritual. If a hero openly dislikes the Patrons, the herald tells them to direct their anger towards the enemy Patron, as is the case with Warden and Venator.17]18] While the heralds can read Warden's emotions, they cannot see his desires.19]

This is the first time in known history that humanity has been able to make contact with the Patrons.20] Such rituals used to be impossible, as well as forbidden, before the Second Maelstrom.21] The orders of the Amber Hand and the Sapphire Flame were supposed to safeguard humanity from the Patrons, but recently are suspected to have changed their objectives.2]

The Patrons are familiar with The Doorman, another extraplanar being. They are happy to see Doorman fighting on their behalf.22]23] The Doorman calls the Patrons "old friends"24] and refers to them as City Mother and Amber King.25]

The Binding of Oathkeeper's multiple endings shows potential wishes granted by the Patrons:

- An answer to one of Abrams' questions regarding the Tome;

- Binding Oathkeeper back to the Tome and allowing Lady Geist to stay young;

- The sacrifice of Johann Jr. in exchange for 200 years of Oathkeeper's complete obedience to Lady Geist;

- The self-sacrifice of Lady Geist in exchange for banishing Oathkeeper from Earth;

- Safely removing Oathkeeper from Geist, but making her return to the natural age she had before their pact.

In addition to the ritual, the Patrons lead skirmishes between their teams in the form of Street Brawl matches.26]

## Trivia

- These names are referenced in the new design of the Patrons, as the Hidden King has "amber hands" and the Archmother's effigy is surrounded by a "sapphire flame".

- Based on game files and voice lines, another proposed rename for the Patrons was the Broken Crown and the Guided Hand. Those names are still used in a few voice lines.

- The Hidden King's voice actor was recast in the Old Gods, New Blood update.

- In Neon Prime, the Patron was called Titan, and the Weakened Patron was the Core. The Patrons are still called "Titans" in the game files.

- The Patron damage to the player is listed as "Condemned by the Patron" in the damage report.

- The text across the old Patron model's reads The Patron's crest

KILOZS BOTAREX UNDT VOXT DIOKOTOS DEOS TOPAX DE OS HOLINAS UXFAXT TOV LIMOT OBERXO

GOL EN TAX FOXIST HARFT DEOS MAX OPUS DEONI SUX TOS DUO POLINUAX DREOX SIDWEG

REBTIS AST MECTONUS MAX DERIKT POLTERMUSON DEFONAL WEKMOXAN GETOX D

- BOTAREX: Possibly a blend of Votum (vow/wish) and Rex (king)

- HARFT: Phonetically resembles Harvest

- MAX OPUS: Similar to Magnum Opus (The Great Work in alchemy and occultism)

- TOS DUO: Contains the root duo (two)

- MECTONUS: A possible blend of Mech (machine) and Chthonic (underworld deity)

- GETOX: Phonetically resembles Gates
