Arin Fairfax, the eldest heir to Fairfax Industries, has been living in hiding for the past 5 years after being shot and left for dead on their 18th birthday. Arin doesn't know who is trying to keep them from taking control of their parent's company... but in the avarice fueled viper's nest that is Fairfax Industries it could be anyone. Taking on the name "Pocket", Arin desperately wants to keep their true identity a secret… not only for their own protection; but because they want to forge their own identity separate from their parents.

Calico was the assassin hired to kill Arin, who survived because the bullet missed their heart.9] It's implied that Enchanter's Satchel also helped Arin survive.10]11] After the assassination attempt, Arin was protected by Mo & Krill12]13] and cut ties with all people from their past life, as they didn't know who could be trusted.14] Taking up a new name as "Pocket", they now try their best to hide their true identity, although their indispensable magic suitcase and designer jacket may attract unwanted attention.15] Some time after the assassination, Pocket left New York before returning to participate in the ritual.16]17] Pocket is 23 years old at the time of the ritual.18]

Pocket believes that their father, Maximilian Fairfax, was behind their attempted assassination.19] Around the time of their assassination, Pocket took their suitcase from their father.10]11] Pocket's magical suitcase, while powerful, was never meant to be used as a weapon,20] and Pocket is trying to keep their suitcase away from Maximilian.21]

Pocket likely has at least one younger sibling, since they're described as the "eldest heir" to Fairfax Industries.

A statue of Walter Fairfax, presumably an ancestor of Pocket, can be found in the garden behind the Baroness Hotel. Walter is depicted as an old man with frogs crawling over his legs. The statue also appears in Pocket's background in the hero select screen.

### Personality

Pocket's years spent on the run have shaped them into a resilient person with strong ideals.22]23] Pocket is not especially talkative,24] and they typically push people away when they try to offer them support.25]26]27] Others often describe them as "sad" and "angsty" as a result.28]29]30] While they have a somewhat gruff exterior, they are more amicable and soft-spoken around those that they trust;31] they also have a cheeky side to their otherwise serious personality.32]33]34]

Pocket hates violence and is only in the ritual as a last resort.35]36]37] Pocket is horrified with themselves whenever they need to use Affliction,38]39]40] trying to justify it as the spirits killing their enemies, not them.41] A few characters notice that Pocket doesn't really want to be fighting in the ritual.42]43]44]

Pocket wants to build a better world.45] Pocket hates those whom they perceive to be complicit with Fairfax's actions and making the world worse.46]47] Pocket also dislikes being seen as a kid.48]

Pocket refuses to take up their old family name until they're no longer ashamed of it.49] That said, Pocket still sees themselves as a Fairfax.50] Pocket worries that, by killing people in the ritual, they're turning more and more into someone they never wanted to be: their father.51]52] Pocket views themselves as the rightful heir of Fairfax Industries.53]

Pocket doesn't expect to win the ritual.54]

### Appearance

Pocket is a slim, light-skinned person of presumably mixed Asian-White descent (based on their facial features and the appearance of their father Maximilian and ancestor Walter). They have brown eyes and short black hair with edges pointed upwards. They wear an expensive but ragged dark green jacket with 6 pockets, fingerless black gloves, an orange scarf with edges resembling skeleton hands, a white shirt with a large collar, orange vest, black necktie, brown pants and black shoes. Their inconspicuous magic suitcase contains many frogs, including a large one that attaches to the ziplines by its tongue. Their weapon is a Nock volley gun, though it behaves as a shotgun.

### Gender Identity

While Pocket's gender identity hasn't been directly stated, they are implied to be non-binary and are exclusively referred to by gender-neutral "they/them" pronouns, including by characters who knew their past identity and call Pocket by their given name "Arin", such as Dynamo and Mina, as well as the Patron's heralds to Mina.55]56]57]

The only known instances in which Pocket is referred to by gendered terms are in The Binding of Oathkeeper, in which their father Maximilian Fairfax refers to Pocket as his "son"58] (while the visual novel is partially outdated and may be rewritten, it is considered canon for the purposes of this wiki), and Mina's backstory indirectly referring to Pocket as her "fiancé".59] Other voice lines referring to Pocket as "he/him" have been removed60] and were confirmed as unintentional by developer Kaori.61] (Notably, another character who deliberately hides her identity, Paradox, refers to herself as a woman and uses "she/her" pronouns.)62]

### Information

Pocket's backstory and abilities have some similarities to the Biblical Plagues of Egypt: the death of the firstborn, the plague of frogs, and the pestilence ( Affliction).

## Character Relationships

### Maximilian Fairfax and Pocket's mother

Pocket believes their father, Maximilian Fairfax, was behind their attempted assassination.19] Pocket wants to stop their father rather than kill him, although Pocket believes that Maximilian would rather Pocket try to kill him.2]

Pocket dislikes their parents and is ashamed of their family's name.2]63]49] While trapped in the Baroness Hotel, Pocket is taunted by The Doorman who tells Pocket that they have no home and that their parents wanted Pocket dead.64]65] Pocket believes their father is "not a good man",66] and their mother "isn't worth saving" and didn't mourn Pocket's death,67]68] and compares her to Yamato.69]

Maximilian seems to know that Pocket survived, as in The Binding of Oathkeeper, he asks Lady Geist if she knows where Pocket is.58] In a seasonal New York Oracle headline, Maximilian donates "a small fortune" to New York orphanages, telling the press that "nobody should be without family on the holidays".70]

### Dynamo

Dynamo is Pocket's godfather. He recognizes Pocket and says he would have helped them if he knew they were alive. Pocket didn't tell Dynamo that they survived because they weren't sure if they could trust him.14]71]

### Mo & Krill

Mo & Krill protected Pocket after their assassination, and they regard each other as family.12]13]72] They love each other.73]

### Calico

Calico was the assassin sent to kill Pocket 5 years before the events of the ritual.18]

Calico enjoys "tying up loose ends" if she kills Pocket during the ritual.74] She also mentions that Maximilian Fairfax will "have questions",75] lining up with Pocket's belief that Maximilian Fairfax was the one who sent Calico to kill Pocket.

Pocket, naturally, dislikes Calico for almost killing them.76]77]

### Mina

Mina was Pocket's fiancée in an arranged marriage.

Mina hates Pocket for "ruining her life", as their assassination meant Mina couldn't become a Fairfax.6]7]8]

It's implied that Mina and Pocket never loved each other, as she only ever cared about getting their last name.78] It's currently unknown what Pocket thinks of Mina.

## Character Interactions

- Despite Pocket's desire to hide their true identity, the other characters seem to be aware of their background and refer to them as an "upstart",79] a "trust fund baby"80] and a "spoiled brat".81]

- Pocket loathes McGinnis due to her work for Fairfax Industries.47]82]46]

- Pocket thinks Yamato would get along with their mother.69]

- Pocket says Vyper is more unstable than their parents63] and is relieved when she is killed, even when both are on the same team.83]

- When Pocket was younger, they idolized Kelvin.84]

- Pocket used to be a big fan of Paradox,85] but now sees her as "just another rich asshole".86]

- Lady Geist recognizes Pocket, but mistakes them for a street urchin. She thinks Pocket's jacket is stolen.15]

- Bebop offers Pocket a place to stay after the ritual.87]

- Warden warns Pocket that their case will be their undoing.91]

- Vindicta believes her life was harder than Pocket's,92] while Pocket believes her rage is misguided.93]94]

- According to Rem, Pocket's clothes smell like the sewer.95]

- Ivy, Rem, and Vyper like Pocket's scarf.96]97]98]

## Removed Lore

- In Pocket's original model, their left hand had an eye tattoo on its back and a decayed skin tissue that glowed with the suitcase. This was removed when their model was updated. However, the current model is still not in a finished state, as it has clipping issues and flat textures.

### Removed character interactions

- Pocket dislikes Wrecker and wants to dismantle Eldritch Tactical Solutions.99]100]

### Neon Prime - Synth

In late Neon Prime/Shadowline, Pocket was named Synth and was later named Fairfax. Synth's model was a modified version of Team Fortress 2's Scout with Holliday's head.

Synth/Fairfax was trying to take over Fairfax Industries, similarly to Pocket.101] However, characters were a lot more hostile towards Fairfax and often mentioned that they were "disowned".102]103]
