The Ziplines (a.k.a.also known as "Transit Lines" or "Hyperlines") are the primary method of long-distance transportation in Deadlock. They run from each team's base down the three lanes, with availability depending on how far a team's Troopers are currently pushed.

The 'Zipline Speed Boost' (F by default) can be activated while riding to grant the 'Zipline Boosted' buff. This ability has the following properties:

- Speed Increase: +80% travel speed

- Duration: 32 seconds

- Cooldown: 360 seconds (6 minutes)

- Initial Unlock Time: 360 seconds (6 minutes) into a match

Destroying a base guardian permanently applies this 80% boost to its corresponding Zipline, turning it into an 'Express Line'.

Additionally, the Movement Powerup increases Zipline Speed by 40-80% (scaling with game time) when active. This applies to both regular speed and boost.

## Combat Interactions

### Usage Lockout

Taking damage from an enemy hero or enemy objective prevents players from attaching to a Zipline for 3 seconds. This includes external damage sources from enemy players, such as a Trooper with Lil Helpers from Rem or a McGinnis Mini Turret. Taking damage from neutral objectives such as Denizens, Sinner's Sacrifice, or enemy troopers (including enemy Rift Troopers) does not prevent a player from attaching to a Zipline.

### Getting Knocked Off

If a hero takes damage from an enemy hero while already riding or attaching to a Zipline, they will be knocked off. This has several effects:

- Damage: 15% of max health (shown as 'Hyperline' in the incoming damage overview; reducible by Spirit Resist).

- Stun: 2.5 seconds. The stun timer does not begin until the hero lands on the ground.

- Fall: The hero falls directly down from the Zipline.

After riding a Zipline for three consecutive seconds, a blue particle effect appears, indicating immunity to being knocked off.

Be aware, as certain damage over time abilities will not knock a hero off a Zipline. For example, Shiv's Serrated Knives, Mirage's Djinn's Mark, and Infernus Afterburn will not knock a hero off after the initial damage is dealt.

## Trivia

- The ziplines have had various names. In Neon Prime they were called hyperlines, in Shadowline they were shadowlines, and now in Deadlock, their official name is transit lines. Some voice lines and UI elements still refer to them by their old names.

- Prior to the 2025-02-25 Update, there were 4 lanes: Yellow (York), Green (Greenwich), Blue (Broadway), and Purple (Park).

- Park Ave. (Green) was previously the Purple lane.

- Before that, the Green lane was Greenwich Lane.

- Even earlier, Greenwich Lane was the Orange lane and was named Orchard.

- Originally, the team's bases were named after Grand Central Station and Penn Station, indicating that they may have been conceived as equivalent to train stations.

- The blue zipline speeds up in the base to account for the extra distance over the Patron's head.
