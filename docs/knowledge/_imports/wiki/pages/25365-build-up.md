Reference extract:

Build-up is a mechanic that causes bullets or melee attacks to accumulate a status effect over multiple hits.

Whenever a hero fires at an enemy, items and abilities with Build-up add a percentage to a meter on that target. Once the meter reaches 100%, the associated status effect is triggered. The progress of a build-up is shown as round icons above the target. Build-up per shot is reduced by damage falloff, dropping to as low as 10% of its base value at maximum range. For most effects, melee damage also applies build-up.

Build-up is a related but distinct mechanic from Stacks. Unlike stacks, which are discrete increments that scale an ongoing effect, build-up functions as a percentage meter that triggers a single status effect upon reaching 100%.

## Items

Items with Build-up apply their percentage to the target through bullet hits. The build-up meter decays if the enemy does not receive any new stacks for 2 seconds, at a rate of 20% per second. Melee attacks count as 1 shot for build-up purposes. Each item's build-up per shot varies by hero, as it is affected by the hero's weapon fire rate and clip size; the exact percentages can be found on each item's page.

### List of items that use Build-up

| Name | Effect on Trigger |

| --- | --- |

| Slowing Bullets | Movement Slow |

| Weighted Shots | Movement Slow |

| Silencer | Silence |

| Inhibitor | Damage Reduction and Healing Reduction |

| Toxic Bullets* | Bleed (Spirit Damage over time) |

| Spiritual Overflow** | Fire Rate, Spirit Power and Spirit Lifesteal |

* Doesn't build-up with melee.

** Spiritual Overflow is a special case: its build-up affects the shooter rather than the target. However, the build-up meter still accumulates on the target being shot, meaning it is impossible to accelerate the process by shooting multiple different targets at once. It is also the only build-up source where the progress is not shown above the target.

## Heroes

Some heroes have abilities that use the Build-up mechanic, accumulating a percentage on the target until the associated effect triggers.

- Infernus' Afterburn: triggers a Burn (Spirit damage over time) effect. Light Melee attacks count as 1 stack; Heavy Melee attacks count as 2 stacks.

- Mina's Love Bites: triggers a Spirit damage proc. Melee attacks do not contribute to build-up.

- Mirage's Djinn's Mark: builds up stacks by shooting the target at certain intervals, triggering a Spirit damage proc either when the timer runs out, when max stacks are reached, or when activated by Mirage. Builds up with a single bullet per stack.

- Silver's Slam Fire (Tier 3): deals bonus Weapon damage if all 3 shots hit the same target.

- Vyper's Lethal Venom (Tier 3): triggers a Spirit damage proc. Can build up with melee and isn't blocked by Petrifying Bola.

Note: Graves' Essence Theft appears visually as a Build-Up, but it's actually a stacking effect, as the effect is applied over time.

## Counters

Several items and abilities can mitigate or counter build-up effects:

- Plated Armor provides a chance to block bullet effects from incoming shots entirely, which includes any build-up effects.

- Abilities and items that disarm the enemy or reduce their fire rate, such as Mo & Krill's Sand Blast, Disarming Hex and Juggernaut.

- Dispel Magic removes any existing build-up applied to the player.

- Debuff Reducer and its upgrades Spellbreaker and Unstoppable

- Blood Tribute

- Weighted Shots

- if the player has no build-up, Counterspell prevents the first shot of build-up. If the player already has a build-up counter, it prevents from triggering when the build-up crosses 100% threshold, but does not prevent the build-up itself.

- Abilities and items that makes the player immune to damage, such as Ivy's Stone Form or Ethereal Shift, will also prevent any build-up on the player.

Notes:

- Metal Skin, despite providing immunity to bullet damage, does not protect against build-up in any way; shots negated by Metal Skin still contribute their full build-up percentage.

- Return Fire does not build up effects against the attacker.
