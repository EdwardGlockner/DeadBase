## 2b - Understanding Oathkeeper

Follows from Chapter 1 if "Try To Understand Oathkeeper Better" was picked.

Lady Geist and Johann Jr. meet up in a café.

Lady Geist: Tell me you have good news.

Johann Jr.: I have news. How good is relative.

Lady Geist: You're very glib today.

Johann Jr.: Well, we're in a public place, so I figured I'd live on the edge.

Lady Geist: What did you learn about Oathkeeper?

Johann Jr.: The notes dad kept on him were meticulous... I still haven't fully digested everything, but there's a promising option emerging.

Lady Geist: Go on.

Johann Jr.: Oathkeeper wasn't named ironically: that thing is like the Patron Saint of Faustian bargains. So, while I'm sure he's pissed that you trapped him for 50 years, there's probably a part of him that's impressed.

Lady Geist: So what's the promising option?

Johann Jr.: I just told you.

Lady Geist: ... I want to make sure I understand: the ward is failing?

Johann Jr.: Yes.

Lady Geist: And there is nothing that you, me, or a minor act of god can do to change that?

Johann Jr.: Correct.

Lady Geist: But I should be encouraged by the fact that I tricked Oathkeeper into bondage?

Johann Jr.: Exactly!

Lady Geist: Out of respect for your father, I'll let you finish that horrid cup of coffee before I kill you.

Johann Jr.: I don't think you're seeing the big picture.

Lady Geist: As soon as the ward fails, I'm as good as dead!

Johann Jr.: Possibly.

Lady Geist: "Possibly"?! For 5 decades, Oathkeeper's had nothing to do but stew on what it can do to me when he's free. Do you know what 50 years of revenge looks like?

Johann Jr.: I don't, and that's why you have a chance... Oma, you are a uniquely cruel woman, and Oathkeeper has to respect that on some level. That thing lives to make deals, so make it another one.

Lady Geist: I have no leverage, why would he... oh my god. Of course... he doesn't know how close he is to breaking free, does he?

Johann Jr.: I don't think so.

Lady Geist: Well then... that changes things, doesn't it?

## 3b - Hunger Strike

Follows from Chapter 2b - Understanding Oathkeeper if "Bluff Oathkeeper Into Finding A New Host" was picked.

Lady Geist: Hello, Fern.

Infernus: Always a pleasure to see you, Lady Geist. Usual booth?

Hank: [in the other room] GO TO HELL!

Lady Geist: Is everything alright?

Infernus: That's a great question... Do you mind if I...

Lady Geist: Of course. I'll show myself up.

Lady Geist sits in an isolated booth.

Oathkeeper: I hunger, Geist.

Lady Geist: You speak as if that's my concern.

Oathkeeper: By the day your beauty fades... I would say it's very much your concern.

Lady Geist: I love it when you posture as if I don't have you under lock and key. You will be fed when I choose to feed you, and not a moment before.

Oathkeeper: My power -

Lady Geist: Is weakening. You think I haven't noticed? Last year, I could kill a vagrant and enjoy the rest of swimsuit season. Now the same hobo would barely last me a drive to The Hamptons. At some point, le jeu n'en vaut pas la chandelle.

Oathkeeper: Now who's posturing?

Lady Geist: What good is eternal life if I spend it in an OSIC oubliette? And we both know that's exactly where I would end up if I gave in to your every whim. No. Your demands are too much... and so I'm given little choice but to... age gracefully.

Oathkeeper: So what? You toss me more scraps for the next 50 years?

Lady Geist: My dear Oathkeeper... The scraps you are fed today will feel like a banquet compared to the crumbs you will be fed in the future.

Oathkeeper: Such conviction. I almost believe you.

Lady Geist: Believe me, don't believe me... Either way, this dynamic of ours is not really benefiting either of us anymore, is it?

Oathkeeper: It sounds like you're about to propose a solution.

Lady Geist: You don't want to be trapped in my arm. I don't want to be killed if I release you.

Oathkeeper: So pay off your debt to me.

Lady Geist: Did you already forget the part where I said I had no intention of obeying your whims?

Oathkeeper: A deal made needs to be honored. It is my nature.

Lady Geist: And it will be. Just not by me.

Oathkeeper: Hm, go on.

Lady Geist: I don't release you. I transfer you. To someone I trust. You get a host for your... regular indulgences...

Oathkeeper: And I don't have a moment of freedom to kill you.

Lady Geist: Exactly.

Oathkeeper: The idea has merit. Do you have someone in mind?

## 4a - Humble Pie

Follows from Chapter 3a - The Gala if "Make Amends With McGinnis" was picked.

Lady Geist arrives at McGinnis' workshop. McGinnis is busy welding something.

Lady Geist: Pardon me, Ms. McGinnis... I believe we started off on the wrong foot.

McGinnis continues working.

McGinnis: I'm busy.

Lady Geist: I understand that, it's just -

McGinnis: None of the other big brains at your little party were able to help you?

Lady Geist: Yes.

McGinnis: Sucks to be you.

Lady Geist: Mistakes were made.

McGinnis: Where's my vase?

Lady Geist: Excuse me?

McGinnis: Rich people love to give away stupid shit when they want to apologize. It's always a vase, or a ceremonial mask, or some silver plated cheese cutting set. So again I ask you: where's my vase?

Lady Geist raises a six-pack of beer.

Lady Geist: It's not quite a vase, but I'm told the proletariat love it.

McGinnis continues working.

Lady Geist: ... That was a joke. Please, c... can we talk?

McGinnis: ... [sighs], come with me.

Lady Geist and McGinnis enter a different part of the lab.

McGinnis: You're not used to not getting your way, are you?

Lady Geist: No.

McGinnis: I appreciate the honesty.

Lady Geist: Can you help me?

McGinnis: Can I? Sure. Will I? Haven't decided yet.

Lady Geist: Why's that?

McGinnis: Well, on the one hand: it's an interesting challenge. On the other, you're just a bad person.

Lady Geist: Your mind is the engine that drives the occult war machine! Within a decade your inventions will claim millions of lives, if they haven't already.

McGinnis: Ye-uh-Sure, but my job is designing weapons. That makes me a monster in the theoretical sense. You're basically a serial killer.

Lady Geist: Eh.

McGinnis: Give me a reason to help you. Something... interesting.

Lady Geist: I have money.

McGinnis: [Scoffs], I said interesting.

Lady Geist ponders for a moment.

Lady Geist: It will likely be 50 years before there's another maelstrom. Do you really want to wait that long before you try to harness the power of a god?

McGinnis: [Chuckles]... now we're talking.

Lady Geist and McGinnis toast with their beers.

Lady Geist: It is!.. And it is slow. And painful. You don't understand what it's like to have your body grow frail and decrepit... for your mind to shrivel... to have others stare at you with nothing but pity... No. I will not die a crone. And if I make Oathkeeper happy? I won't have to.

Lady Geist: Don't be stupid. You are a part of this.

Lady Geist: Darling. You don't have a choice.

Johann Jr.: Go to hell, Oma...

Fairfax: So. Oathkeeper... Are you ready to change the world?
